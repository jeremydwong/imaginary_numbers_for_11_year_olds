# intro

That's the journey. Look how far the idea travelled — and notice that **not once** did we need to believe in {{i}} because an equation ordered us to. Each step, it paid its way.

The whole story in five sentences:

- **The bad start:** opening with {{x² + 1 = 0}} makes {{i}} look like a name for answers that aren't there.
- **The real start:** cubics like {{x³ = 15x + 4}} have ordinary real roots that Cardano's formula can reach *only* by passing through {{√−121}} — imaginary numbers do essential work, then cancel.
- **What {{i}} really is:** the **90° rotation**, so {{i² = −1}} is geometry, not decree; multiplying complex numbers **scales by length and adds angles.**
- **The crown jewel:** "velocity = {{i}} × position" forces motion around the unit circle, so {{e^{iθ} = cos θ + i sin θ}} — and {{e^{iπ} = −1}} is just a half-lap.
- **Up a dimension:** **quaternions** carry that idea into 3D rotation (at the cost of {{ij ≠ ji}}), and run every robot, spacecraft, and game engine.
- **All the way down:** quantum amplitudes are **rotating complex arrows** you add and then square — and that's where interference, and hence chemistry and electronics, comes from.

:::callout color=gold
The unifying sentence for the whole site: **imaginary numbers are the mathematics of rotation, and rotation turns out to be everywhere** — hidden inside cubic formulas, obvious in the plane, essential in 3D, and woven into the fabric of matter.
:::end
